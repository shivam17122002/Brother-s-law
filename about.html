What is Brother’s Law?

It’s a tongue-in-cheek observation about how we often find everything except what we’re looking for — in drawers, in notes, and on the internet. This site documents the law, gives examples, and suggests ways to beat it.

Mission

Make people smile and also think about better search habits, calmer recall, and cleaner organization.

How to Use

  • Start at the Home page for the definition and examples.
  • Visit FAQs for quick answers.
  • Share your story via the form — we might feature anonymized highlights.
